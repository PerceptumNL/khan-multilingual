Technology

Khan Academy is ready to go with a reliable internet connection and a modern web browser.

Since beginning our work with schools in late 2010, we've seen schools successfully use a variety of devices and browsers. The most important things to have in place are accounts for logging in to KA, computers and devices, and internet connectivity.

Log-in / accounts

For a large number of accounts, we recommend Google Apps for Education (described in the accounts section). Google Apps for Education is the only way to register students under age 13 on Khan Academy (with appropriate parental consent).

Today, for all users, we support Google and Facebook authentication; we do not have our own authentication service.

Computers and devices

Khan Academy runs entirely in your browser, and there is no special software required to use the site, so you can access KA with any device that runs a modern internet browser. In the schools we work with we have seen the whole gamut including laptops, Chromebooks, Netbooks, iPads, and other tablet devices.

We recommend having a 1:1 student-to-computer ratio in the classroom when using KA. At many schools, classroms share laptop carts. We've also seen some schools employ an open-access policy that permits students to bring in their own devices.

Internet connectivity

Classrooms should have adequate access-point availability and bandwidth:

  • Access points For full access to the site, make sure your wireless can accommodate every student working simultaneously on Khan Academy
  • Connection speed (bandwidth) Khan Academy requires a broadband connection (~1.5 Mbps for a single machine viewing standard definition video) to watch videos, practice using exercises, and view data

YouTube filtering

If you'd like your students to be able to watch Khan Academy videos and other educational content without giving them full access to YouTube, you can sign up for YouTube for Schools. Find full details on setting up YouTube for Schools here.
