Projects in the classroom

Projects enable students to gain deeper conceptual understandings, team building skills, and valuable inquiry-driven experiences. We encourage teachers to use projects alongside Khan Academy to 1) free up class time, 2) make sure that students have the necessary skills to be able to do the projects, and 3) enable students to apply their skills to relevant, real world problems.

In our pilot classrooms, we’ve seen teachers use projects in the following ways:

  • Hooks that whet students’ appetites to learn more
  • Self-directed investigations of a math concept
  • Creative applications of math skills
  • Exploratory, inquiry-based experiences

Share your projects

If you have any project ideas that you would like to share, please send them to us at implementations@khanacademy.org and use this template to illustrate how you have used the project alongside KA exercises and videos.

Pilot a project based curriculum

While we have seen excellent teachers use projects in conjunction with KA, we are especially interested in seeing how KA works when integrated into a project-based curriculum. If you already teach a project-based math curriculum and have a vision of how to blend KA into your classroom, please apply to our pilot.
